I study how people's responses are shaped — by question wording, context, and the limits of what they're willing to say — and I build transparent computational tools for turning messy human responses into interpretable evidence.

01 / About

I am a researcher working at the intersection of measurement methodology and computational social science. My work asks how researchers can turn complex social data — surveys, open-ended text, and digital traces — into reliable evidence without hiding the uncertainty and interpretive decisions involved.

I was trained in survey methodology and statistical monitoring, and my earlier work focused on response quality, measurement error, and data collection processes. I now extend this measurement perspective to computational text analysis and AI-assisted coding workflows, building tools that make social science analysis more transparent, auditable, and reproducible.

02 / Research

Research Areas

My work connects measurement methodology, computational text analysis, and substantive social science — asking how we can build more transparent and reliable analytical workflows for complex social data.

Measurement Quality

Statistical monitoring of data collection, response quality assessment, and error detection in survey and computational pipelines.

Computational Text Analysis

Model-assisted coding of open-ended text, interpretable annotation workflows, and reliability assessment for AI-generated codes.

Latent Structure & Heterogeneity

Mixture models, latent-class analysis, and structured heterogeneity detection in attitudinal and behavioral data.

Survey Methodology

Multivariate process control, nonparametric monitoring, and scalable quality indicators for ongoing data collection.

Social Inequality

Gender attitudes, hidden patterns in attitudinal surveys, and mechanism-based explanations from individual-level data.

Open & Reproducible Science

Building research tools that make analytical decisions auditable, shareable, and reproducible across teams.

04 / Projects

Software & Research Projects

Tools and studies I'm building — focused on making computational social science workflows more transparent and reproducible.

Featured · Active Development

OpenCodebook

A Python framework for transparent, auditable, model-assisted coding of open-ended social science text. Researchers define structured codebooks in YAML; local open-source LLMs apply the codes; the pipeline produces structured outputs with built-in reliability diagnostics. Currently piloted on the GLES 2025 German election survey — analyzing how reliably different models code citizens' "most important political problem" responses.

Python Ollama YAML GLES 2025 Measurement Quality Open Source
View on GitHub ↗ Try the demo ↗

GLES MIP Reliability Pilot

A measurement quality study using OpenCodebook to analyze inter-model agreement on 400 stratified open-ended responses from the GLES Rolling Cross-Section 2025. Examines how coding reliability varies by response complexity and issue domain.

Gender Attitude Latent Structures

Research on hidden heterogeneity in gender attitude surveys using mixture models and latent-class analysis. Published in Sociological Methods & Research (2025). Currently extending to open-ended text responses.
